INTRODUCING “ONE CHILD PER TIME”

The “One Child Per Time” initiative is aimed at reducing the number of out-of-school children in Nigerian communities by giving each child the opportunity to get a quality education in every state in the country irrespective of their background, religion, or culture. Partner Mobile is focused on spreading love and hope to disadvantaged children who are struggling to cater to their basic educational needs.

Learn More

According to Global Citizen, Nigeria has the world’s largest number of Out-of-School Children.

A survey conducted by the United Nations Children's Fund (UNICEF) indicates that the population of out-of-school children in Nigeria has risen from 10.5 million to 13.2 million.

What these statistics mean is that “one in every five of the world’s out-of-school children is in Nigeria.”

Why does quality education matter to a child?

When we invest in high-quality early childhood education, it helps make sure that we are providing the best opportunities for children to grow and learn. By being in a good school environment, the foundation of a child is set for them to be successful in school, work and life.

What we aim to achieve with this project?

74 children from all 36 states in Nigeria including the FCT to get quality education.

6 schools in the six geo-political zones of Nigeria. Each adopted school will receive infrastructural improvements.

Educational materials including exercise books, pens, textbooks, lunch boxes, etc to students across several schools in Nigeria.

Provide support to the parents and caregivers of out-of-school children in order to support their learning capability and a safe return to school.
